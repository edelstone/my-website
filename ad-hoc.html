Quick intro

I’m Michael Edelstone, a designer living in San Marcos, Texas and currently working on product at BrickFTP. Before that I led design and user experience at Texas State University.

I aim to be be your next UX Designer, and I wrote this letter from scratch for your consideration.

So, why am I right for this?

I’m pretty excited about the civic nature of your work, and I check just about all of the boxes on your job posting (including the preferred ones). Let me get specific:

  1. I do research and discovery: I have deep experience leading customer focus groups and turning those insights into actions and well-received outcomes. I like tools like HotJar and good old-fashioned surveys to learn about who is using the product, and why.
  2. I make design libraries: I’m happy adding to existing brands or building my own. In fact, I’m the architect of both the Action Verb brand and Texas State’s digital brand; the latter is a featured case study on my portfolio, if you’re interested.
  3. I’m good with wireframes and high fidelity: I’ve grown fond of using Balsamiq and InVision for quick mockups. I’ve also been using Sketch since its inception, and I’m equally at home using Atom, GitHub, and the command line.
  4. I’m get the front end: I contribute directly to the BrickFTP React codebase daily. And that’s my HTML/CSS in production on txstate.edu, brickftp.com, and actionverb.com. I’m happily up-to-date on flexbox, CSS animations, Sass, Bootstrap, BEM, and PostCSS.
  5. I’m an a11y: I advocate for accessibility and strive for at least WCAG AA standards on everything I make. Texas State University required AAA conformance, so I’m comfortable even with the strictest mandates.
  6. I’m remote-ready: I’ve been working remotely in a highly-autonomous role for over a year now, and I’m convinced that I’m at my best and most-focused in my home studio or local coffee shop. Tools like Slack, Basecamp, and Zoom are second nature, and I’m happy to meet in person at regular intervals.
