Quick intro 👋

I’m Michael Edelstone, a designer living in San Marcos, Texas, and currently making things at BrickFTP. Before that, I led design and user experience at Texas State University.

I want to be your next designer, and I wrote this letter in an attempt to impress you.

So, why am I right for this? 🤔

I've been collaborating on useful websites and SaaS products for the past eight years, and I plan grow a ton in the near future. Let me get specific:

  1. I've shipped complex web apps: BrickFTP is a file sharing app made exclusively for businesses. I was the principal designer for our recent switch from a legacy Rails UI to a React front end. I'm responsible for all of the UI (including, color, typography, layout, forms, modals, slideshows, error messages, upload dialogs, mobile view, transactional pages, and onboarding) and most of the UX (here are a couple of examples).
  2. I get developers and the front end: I contribute directly to the BrickFTP codebase daily, and that’s my HTML/CSS in production on txstate.edu, and brickftp.com. When I don't know how to build something myself, I rely on a refined, understandable design process – using Sketch, Balsamiq, and InVision – that developers love. I’m active on GitHub and am happily up-to-date on flexbox, CSS animations, Sass, WCAG 2.0, Git, and basic JavaScript.
  3. I get marketing and brands, too: I'm the architect of Texas State's digital brand. I also designed and built the institution's first responsive homepage, which is viewed over a million times each month, and even wrote the script for a nationally-televised ad campaign that accompanied the release. At BrickFTP, I recently redesigned their web presence, with a focus on brand identity, increased conversions, and strict accessibility compliance.
  4. I focus on the customer: When we decided to overhaul Texas State's CMS, I led several focus groups to uncover insights from our large community of web publishers, later converting those ideas into some well-received outcomes. I've continued this trend at BrickFTP, building stronger connections with over 1,700 customers through surveys, one-on-one conversations, split testing, and passive tools like HotJar and Google Analytics.
  5. I’m remote-ready: I’ve been working remotely with a diverse team for a while now, and I’m convinced that I’m at my best in my home office or local coffee shop. Tools like Slack and Zoom are second nature, and I’m happy to meet in person regularly. Coworkers consider me honest, humorous, and expertly prepared with inspirational GIFs.
  6. I know WordPress: Alright, I'm not a total unicorn – the truth is that I don't know WordPress very well. I'm always down for a challenge, and hopefully the above does a good job of demonstrating my initiative and desire to master your toolset ASAP.
