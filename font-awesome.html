Quick intro 👋

I’m Michael Edelstone, a designer living in San Marcos, Texas and most recently building products at Kuali. Before that, I led design initiatives at Files.com and Texas State University.

I want to be your next designer, and I wrote this letter from scratch for your consideration.

So, why am I right for this? 🤔

My mom was a leader in non-profits for 40 years before her recent retirement, so I have a strong appreciation for your mission and impact. In addition to being down for the cause, I also bring a sincere optimism to the workplace along with a refined process that developers and product people love. Let me get specific:

  1. I'm versatile and flexible: During my time at Kuali I grew fond of Balsamiq and InVision for quick mockups, jumping over to Figma for increased fidelity, and even coding an HTML/CSS prototype if it makes sense. I was also a leader on the design systems team, contributing strongly in the areas of layout, higher-order components and accessibility.
  2. I focus on the customer: I have extensive experience leading usability testing, focus groups, and open-ended conversations with real people. At Kuali I tested product features weekly, managed tools like Airtable, Hotjar, and Google Analytics, and developed rigorous moderated testing plans that energized the entire team.
  3. I've taken apps from conception to launch: I was the principal designer and CSS developer at Files.com during the switch from a legacy Rails UI to a React front end. I’m responsible for nearly the entire interface, including color, typography, desktop and mobile layouts, forms, modals, slideshows, error messages, upload dialogs, transactional pages, and onboarding.
  4. I get engineers and the front end: I’ve been writing standards-compliant markup since 2009. I manage some popular open-source projects on GitHub, and am happily up-to-date on flexbox, Sass, media queries, CSS animations, Git, and SVG.
  5. I’m a natural communicator: Coworkers and customers consider me honest, humorous, and expertly prepared with inspirational GIFs. I invented Texas State University's digital language and I’m also an accomplished UX writer, authoring thousands of words in the product interfaces and marketing materials everywhere I’ve worked.
  6. I’m remote-ready: I've spent the bulk of my career being the primary design voice on small, distributed teams. Tools like Slack and Zoom are second nature, and I’m happy to visit you regularly in New York City (when life gets back to normal).
