Introduction

I’m Michael Edelstone, a designer living in San Marcos, Texas and most recently building products at an agency named SPR. Before that I led design initiatives at Balto, Kuali, and Texas State University.

I want to be your next designer and I wrote this letter from scratch for your consideration.

So, why am I right for this?

I bring a sincere optimism to the workplace along with a refined design process that developers love. Coworkers consider me honest, humorous, and expertly prepared with inspirational GIFs.

Let me get specific:

  1. I’m versatile and flexible: This won’t be my first rodeo as the sole designer on a small team. I’m equally comfortable managing a marketing site, imagining a new feature, writing production-quality code, chatting with a stakeholder, or polishing a high-fidelity prototype. I’ll probably have the shortest onboarding for a designer you’ll ever experience.
  2. I get engineers and the front end: I’ve been writing standards-compliant markup since 2009 and working inside React codebases since 2017. I manage some popular open-source projects on GitHub, and am up-to-date on flexbox, Sass, media queries, CSS animations, JSX, and SVG. I enjoy contributing to code reviews and fully expect to be critiqued myself.
  3. I focus on enterprise software: I purposefully seek out work in the B2B and enterprise space because I enjoy the challenges and the consumers who use the product. I don’t chase trends, instead favoring tested and reliable methods that simultaneously meet the expectations of power users, corporate leaders, and technophobes alike.
  4. I’m remote-ready: I’ve been working exclusively with distributed teams since 2017, long before it was cool. Tools like Slack, Zoom, and Basecamp are second nature and I’m happy to visit you in Bozeman at regular intervals.
