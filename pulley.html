Introduction

I’m Michael Edelstone, a designer living in San Marcos, Texas and most recently building products at an agency named SPR. Before that I led design initiatives at Balto, Kuali, and Texas State University.

I want to be your next designer and I tailored this letter for your consideration.

So, why am I right for this?

I bring a sincere optimism to the workplace along with a refined, understandable process that developers love. Let me get specific:

  1. I’m versatile and flexible: This won’t be my first rodeo as a design generalist with senior-level responsibilities. I’m equally comfortable imagining a new feature, building a design system, advocating for accessibility, chatting with a stakeholder, or polishing a high-fidelity prototype. I’ll probably have the quickest onboarding for a designer you’ll ever experience.
  2. I get engineers and the front end: I’ve been writing standards-compliant markup since 2009 and working inside React/Angular codebases since 2017. I manage some popular open-source projects, and am up-to-date on grid, flexbox, Sass, media queries, and SVG. I love the dev side but I’m happy staying away from the code if it suits you; no ego here.
  3. I focus on data-intensive enterprise software: I purposefully seek out projects in the B2B space because I enjoy the technical challenges and tricky data displays that accompany them. I don’t chase trends. I favor tested and reliable methods that simultaneously meet the expectations of power users, corporate leaders, and technophobes alike.
  4. I’m remote-ready and a great communicator: I’ve been working exclusively with distributed teams since 2017, all of them small, energetic startups. Tools like Slack, Zoom, and Basecamp are second nature, and co-workers consider me honest, humorous, and expertly prepared with inspirational GIFs.
